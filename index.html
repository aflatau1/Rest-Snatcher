The NYC Reservation Hunter

How to land the hardest tables
in New York City

The hardest reservations in the city aren't won at dinner — they're won the second the booking window opens. Here's the playbook, the drop calendar, and a reminder system so you never miss a release.

The Playbook

Eight tactics that actually work
01

Know the exact drop

Most tough rooms release a fixed number of days out (often 14, 28, or 30) at a precise time — frequently midnight or 10:00 AM ET. Booking is a race measured in seconds, not minutes.

02

Be logged in & loaded early

Open Resy/Tock 60 seconds before the drop, payment and profile saved, party size pre-selected. Refresh at T-minus 5 seconds, not after.

03

Go off-peak

5:00 PM and 9:45 PM on a Tuesday clear far faster than 7:30 PM Saturday. Solo and 2-tops have radically better odds than 4+.

04

Two devices, two windows

Phone app and desktop browser hitting the same drop. Whichever confirms first wins; release the other immediately.

05

Notify lists & waitlists

Turn on Resy Notify and Tock waitlists. Cancellations flood back 24–48h before service and on rainy days.

06

Walk in & sit at the bar

Many "impossible" rooms hold the bar and counter for walk-ins. Arrive at open, give a name, get a drink nearby.

07

Be a regular elsewhere in the group

Hospitality groups (Major Food, Quality Branded, Frenchette) reward loyalty. A relationship at one room opens others.

08

Set a reminder for the drop

The single highest-leverage move: a calendar alarm minutes before the window opens. Build yours below.
